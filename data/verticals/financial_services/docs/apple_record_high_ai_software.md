# Apple shares close at record high after company unveils AI software

**Category:** Technology / Tech
**Author:** Ashley Capoot
**Published:** Tue, Jun 11 2024 11:14 AM EDT
**Updated:** Tue, Jun 11 2024 4:01 PM EDT
**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2024/06/11/apple-shares-pop-to-record-high-after-company-unveils-ai-software.html)

> *Note: CNBC's byline currently shows Ashley Capoot; the user-supplied source listing credited Kif Leswing.*

## Key Points

- Apple shares notched a new record high of around $207 per share on Tuesday, besting the previous record from December.
- The company unveiled its long-awaited push into AI at its annual developer conference on Monday.
- Analysts believe the new AI features will drive consumers to upgrade their devices since the tools are only available on newer models.

---

Apple shares closed up 7% to a new record high of $207.15 per share on Tuesday, a day after the company announced its long-awaited push into artificial intelligence at its annual developer conference.

Apple introduced a range of new AI features during the event, including an overhaul of its voice assistant Siri, integration with OpenAI's ChatGPT, a range of writing assistance tools and new image generation capabilities. The company calls the suite of features "Apple Intelligence."

With Tuesday's share move, Apple bested its previous record from Dec. 14. The company's developer conference came as a welcome sign for investors who have been watching to see how Apple plans to compete in the AI race alongside rivals like Microsoft and Alphabet.

Analysts from Morgan Stanley said Apple's AI features strongly position the company with "the most differentiated consumer digital agent." Additionally, the analysts believe that the rollout will spur an iPhone upgrade cycle, since Apple Intelligence will only run on the iPhone 15 Pro and Pro Max and on devices powered by M1 chips or newer.

They said Apple will still have to deliver when the AI features are first available in the fall, but they think the "building blocks are in place for a return to growth and more sustainable multiple."

> "Following a WWDC that delivered on the key details we were focused on heading into the event, we have even greater conviction that Apple is on the cusp of a multi-year product refresh cycle."
> — Morgan Stanley analysts

Bank of America analysts were also optimistic about Apple's announcements, saying the features will lead to "an upgrade cycle for AI-enabled IntelliPhones." The analysts maintained their buy rating on the stock.

Evercore analysts said they came away from the conference with "increased confidence" in Apple's AI strategy, particularly because they believe it will push users to upgrade their devices.

> "Restricting Apple Intelligence to iPhones sold within the last year adds to our conviction that AI can help kick off an iPhone super cycle."
> — Evercore analysts, in a note Monday
